# Entry 024 Something I Still Wrestle With

One thing I do still wrestle with sometimes is a kind of imposter syndrome around the way I build.

Even when I try to reason my way out of it, it still shows up.

A lot of it comes down to the fact that I use AI to fill in real gaps that I have.

There are parts of building that come naturally to me, and parts that really don’t.

The thinking side comes naturally.
The systems side comes naturally.
Pattern recognition comes naturally.
Direction, structure, and seeing how things connect come naturally.

But getting all of that out of my head cleanly has never been as easy.

Writing has always been harder for me than thinking.
Explaining things in a way that feels as clear as they are in my head has always been harder than actually understanding them.
Sometimes my mind moves faster than my ability to translate it into words.
Sometimes I know what I mean and still struggle to get it out in a way that looks polished, clean, or confident from the outside.

That gap has been part of my life for a long time.

So using tools to help bridge it is not really new for me.

Spelling correction.
Grammar correction.
Speech to text.
Anything that helps me get from what I can see internally to something I can actually express or work with more effectively.

AI is a much more powerful version of that same pattern.

It helps me write.
It helps me understand things more deeply.
It helps me work through code, documentation, and technical ideas that I might not have been able to push as far on my own.
It helps me extend what I can do.

And I do think that is a very human thing.

At least it feels that way to me.

It feels like using external tools to augment my intelligence, compensate for weaker surfaces, and broaden what I am able to build in the world. Not replacing my mind. Extending it. Giving shape to things I might otherwise struggle to bring fully into the open.

But even knowing that, the imposter feeling still hits sometimes.

Because I can still imagine what other people might think.

That I’m not really building.
That I’m just orchestrating.
That I’m just copying and pasting.
That it doesn’t count because I’m not the one hand-writing every line of code or every sentence of documentation from scratch.

And I think part of what makes that feeling hard is that it latches onto something real.

It is true that I use AI heavily.
It is true that I use it to help me with areas where I have less strength.
It is true that there are things I am able to do now because these tools exist that I probably could not have done the same way before.

That part is real.

But I also think something else is real.

The direction is still mine.
The taste is still mine.
The judgment is still mine.
The persistence is still mine.
The system shaping is still mine.
The decision to keep going, to keep refining, to keep caring whether something is true or hollow — that is still mine too.

So I live in this strange middle space sometimes.

I know I’m doing something real.
I know the work matters to me.
I know I’m not passive in it.

But I also still wrestle with the fear that other people might only see the tool and decide that none of it counts.

I don’t have a perfect resolution to that.

I think I’m still working through it.

What I do know is that AI has become part of how I bridge the distance between what I can perceive, what I can understand, and what I can actually bring into the world.

And even if I’m still figuring out how to hold that identity cleanly in my own mind, I’m not going to stop building.
